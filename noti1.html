Ready to read other people’s minds

What about an algorithm to read other people’s minds? As you already know, we translate ideas into reality, and we’ve done something nobody has done before.

The software development team of Appsmatu has devised an algorithm that processes and decodes the words a person hears and thinks about. This is possible undergoing a functional magnetic resonance imaging, an efficient and non-intrusive method.

The methodology of the algorithm is quite simple and practical. Instead of stopping to analyze the neurons, the magnetic resonances measure the changes in blood flow within the brain, clear indicators of the level of brain activity. As we said, the physical signals of the blood move much slower than the neurons, so the system does not translate word by word what someone thinks, but rather analyzes the semantic meaning of people's thoughts.

Obviously, the software would need hours and hours of analysis and brain scanning before it could decipher what a person is thinking at any moment, so it seems that the time when this tool can be used effectively and reliably is a long way off. .

We’re already working with the software and the results have been successful so far. The program just needs 15 minutes to decipher what a person is thinking about at any moment, and the results are realistic. “It decodes exactly what I’m thinking about”, as Kevin Torres from Appsmatu has confirmed.
